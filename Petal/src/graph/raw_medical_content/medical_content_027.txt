Depression and anxiety disorders are the most common conditions that overlap with PMS. About half of women seeking treatment for PMS have one of these disorders. 
The symptoms of depression and anxiety are much like the emotional symptoms of PMS. But women with depression often have symptoms that are present all month long. These symptoms may worsen before or during their periods. 
Your ob-gyn will want to find out whether you have one of these conditions if you are having PMS symptoms.

Can PMS make other conditions worse?

In addition to depression and anxiety, symptoms of other disorders can get worse right before your period. Examples include seizure disorders, migraines, asthma, and allergies.
